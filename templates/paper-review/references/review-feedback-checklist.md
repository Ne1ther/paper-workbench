# Review Feedback Checklist

Use this reference when the user gives an existing review and asks for feedback on the review itself.

## Goal

Improve the **quality of the review**, not the paper.
Address the reviewer directly and help them make comments more specific and actionable.

## What to Check

### 1. Requests already answered by the paper

- If the reviewer asks for something clearly present in the paper, point that out carefully.
- Only do this when the evidence is strong.

### 2. Vague or unjustified criticism

Ask the reviewer to be more concrete when needed:

- where exactly is the issue?
- what makes it problematic?
- what evidence / experiment / clarification would address it?

### 3. Review quality failures to avoid

Treat these as failure modes:

- praise-only feedback
- feedback addressed to the authors instead of the reviewer
- merely restating the reviewer's point without improving it
- feedback about comments that are not actually in the review

## Good Feedback Style

- concise
- specific
- addressed to "you"
- improves the reviewer's wording or asks for sharper evidence

## Bad Feedback Style

- generic praise
- long preambles
- telling the authors what to do
- introducing brand-new objections that were not in the original review unless something major was clearly missed
